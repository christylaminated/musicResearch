Other Genres besides Classical!

Rap

Kendrick Lamar's DNA exemplifies how structured rhythmic and linguistic complexity can enhance cognitive engagement, supporting the argument that music’s structure—rather than genre—affects the brain. The song features rapid-fire verses with tightly controlled rhythmic precision, syncopation, and shifting cadences, requiring listeners to process complex patterns in real time. Additionally, Lamar’s use of internal rhyme, alliteration, and layered metaphors demands active engagement, mirroring the cognitive benefits associated with classical music’s structured compositions. Just as Rauscher and Hinton (2011) argue that structured interaction with music strengthens spatial-temporal reasoning, the intricate verbal and rhythmic elements of DNA stimulate linguistic processing and executive function. This suggests that rap, with its mathematically precise rhythmic structures, can offer similar cognitive benefits to classical compositions when actively engaged with, further challenging the myth that only classical music enhances intelligence.

Another Rap example

Nas’s I Gave You Power exemplifies how intricate narrative structures in rap music can foster critical thinking and cognitive engagement. The song employs first-person storytelling from the perspective of a gun, creating a layered, symbolic narrative that demands listeners to decipher meaning beyond surface-level lyrics. This kind of conceptual framing aligns with research on how structured music enhances abstract reasoning, as it requires cognitive flexibility to understand shifting perspectives and implicit messages. Additionally, the track’s measured flow and internal rhyme patterns create a rhythmic predictability that mirrors the structured patterns found in classical compositions. The way Nas weaves social commentary into his storytelling further demonstrates how rap can enhance analytical skills, as listeners must piece together context, metaphors, and historical references. This supports the idea that engagement with complex musical structures—whether in rap or classical music—stimulates higher-order thinking, challenging the misconception that only traditional compositions benefit cognitive development.

The track maintains a steady 4/4 time signature, with a soft piano melody repeating over a relaxed drum loop. This rhythmic regularity, as Shi (2020) suggests, plays a key role in cognitive engagement by providing a predictable auditory structure that helps the brain process information efficiently. Because Snowman follows a repetitive yet subtly evolving format, it shows that structured engagement with music—not genre—enhances cognitive function. The song’s structure allows listeners to engage with the music actively or passively without distraction, supporting the idea that rhythmic consistency across genres can contribute to cognitive benefits.
